Market Insights

Key Takeaway

The Poland market shows a distinct winter and fall seasonality with peaks in January-February and October-November. Q4 represents the highest travel query period, accounting for over 38% of annual queries.

Seasonal Pattern

The Poland market demonstrates a distinct seasonal pattern with hotel guests peaking in August (4,724) and September (4,685), with November (2,715) as a strong third. For travel queries, the highest peak occurs in January (11.41), followed by February (9.20). The lowest performance for both metrics occurs during the summer months (May-July).

Seasonal Distribution

  • Winter (Dec-Feb): 33% of annual travel queries
  • Spring (Mar-May): 18% of annual travel queries
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): 15% of annual travel queries
  • Fall (Sep-Nov): 34% of annual travel queries

Peak Performance

  • Top Month (Travel Queries): January (11.41) in 2024; November (18.75) projected for 2025
  • Top Month (Hotel Guests): August (4,724)
  • Top Month (Flight Searches): December (3,203)
  • Secondary Peak: January-February

Implications for Marketing

The distinct winter and fall seasonality of the Poland market suggests that marketing efforts should be concentrated in Q2 and early Q3 to capture the peak booking window for winter travel. The 62-day average booking window indicates that campaigns should be launched approximately 2 months before the target travel period.

Strategic Opportunity

The summer months (June-August) represent an opportunity for growth, as they currently account for only 15% of annual travel queries. Targeted summer campaigns highlighting Abu Dhabi's indoor attractions and luxury hotel experiences could help balance the seasonal distribution.

2025 Forecast Insight

The 2025 forecast shows a significant shift in peak travel query month from January to November across all scenarios, with November projected to reach 18.75 in the moderate scenario (a 98% increase from November 2024). This dramatic shift suggests a substantial evolution in the Poland market's seasonal pattern, with the peak period extending further into Q4 and showing much stronger performance.

Key Takeaway

Strategic recommendations for the Poland market focus on addressing authenticity concerns, promoting cultural experiences, enhancing safety perception, leveraging the decrease in restrictive laws/customs perception, and developing targeted seasonal campaigns.

Address Authenticity Concerns

Implement an "Authentic Abu Dhabi" campaign to counter the perception of inauthenticity (26%).

  • Feature authentic cultural experiences and heritage sites
  • Create a dedicated Polish microsite showcasing local traditions and customs
  • Partner with Polish cultural influencers to document authentic experiences
  • Develop content highlighting interactions with local Emiratis

Projected outcome: Reduce "not authentic enough" perception by 8-10pp

Safety Perception Enhancement

Launch a "Safe Destination" campaign to address the increase in safety concerns (22%, +1pp).

  • Highlight Abu Dhabi's safety rankings and statistics
  • Feature testimonials from Polish visitors about safety experiences
  • Create content comparing safety metrics with popular destinations
  • Partner with Polish travel insurance providers for special rates

Projected outcome: Reduce "safety concerns" perception by 6-8pp

Seasonal Campaign Strategy

Develop targeted seasonal campaigns to address the significant seasonality in the Poland market.

  • Create a "Summer Escape" campaign highlighting indoor attractions and activities
  • Develop winter-focused content emphasizing the contrast with Polish winter
  • Implement shoulder season promotions to balance demand throughout the year
  • Partner with Polish tour operators for seasonal package deals

Projected outcome: Increase summer travel queries by 25-30% and balance seasonal distribution

Destination Differentiation

Address the "prefer other destinations" barrier (23%, -6pp) through targeted differentiation.

  • Create comparative content highlighting Abu Dhabi's unique offerings vs. competitors
  • Develop "Only in Abu Dhabi" campaign showcasing unique experiences
  • Highlight value proposition compared to other luxury destinations
  • Partner with Polish celebrities for destination endorsements

Projected outcome: Further reduce "prefer other destinations" perception by 5-7pp
